# Luxury Retail Digital Transformation Strategy: Prada Australia Case

## Executive Summary

This project is a consulting-style business analytics case focused on the digital transformation of Prada Australia’s retail operations.

The project evaluates how RFID, Augmented Reality (AR), and AI-powered CRM tools can improve operational efficiency, customer engagement, and sales performance in luxury retail. The analysis combines strategic consulting frameworks, qualitative store-level insights, time series forecasting, clustering, scenario modeling, and implementation planning.

Due to confidentiality and NDA considerations, this public repository does not include the original report, presentation, raw data, interview transcripts, internal KPI tables, or store-level revenue values. The README summarizes the project scope, methodology, analytical logic, and business value in a public-safe format.

## Business Problem

Luxury retail is increasingly shaped by digital expectations. Customers expect faster service, stronger personalization, seamless omnichannel interaction, and more immersive in-store experiences.

The business problem analyzed in this project was:

**How can digital technologies and AI tools improve operational and sales efficiency in Prada Australia stores?**

The project identified several high-level operational challenges:

- manual and time-consuming inventory processes;
- limited automation in client relationship management;
- inconsistent customer personalization;
- limited use of immersive retail technologies;
- revenue volatility across store formats;
- uneven digital adoption at the store level.

---

## Project Objective

The objective was to develop a data-driven digital transformation strategy for Prada Australia.

The project focused on three technology pillars:

| Technology | Strategic Role |
|-----------|----------------|
| RFID | Improve inventory visibility, stock accuracy, replenishment speed, and operational control |
| AI-powered CRM | Improve client personalization, retention, reactivation, and targeted communication |
| Augmented Reality | Improve customer engagement, product discovery, conversion, and in-store experience |

The final output was a consulting roadmap with phased implementation, risk analysis, KPI logic, and scenario-based revenue impact assessment.

## Research Questions

The project addressed the following questions:

1. What operational inefficiencies limit store performance and customer experience?
2. Which digital technologies are most relevant for luxury retail transformation?
3. How can RFID, AR, and AI-powered CRM support operational and sales efficiency?
4. Which store types should be prioritized for pilot testing and phased rollout?
5. How can the impact of digital transformation be measured using practical KPIs?

## Time Series Analysis

A major part of the project was focused on store-level revenue forecasting.

### Analytical Goal

The goal was to understand how revenue evolves over time and to establish a baseline forecast before modeling digital transformation scenarios.

### Methods Used

| Method | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| Time Series Visualization | Identify trends and seasonal patterns |
| ADF Test | Check stationarity |
| Differencing | Stabilize non-stationary series |
| ACF / PACF | Support ARIMA order selection |
| ARIMA / SARIMA | Model trend and seasonality |
| Ljung-Box Test | Diagnose residual autocorrelation |
| ARCH / GARCH Testing | Detect and model volatility clustering |
| 12-month Forecasting | Build baseline revenue scenarios |

### Why It Matters

Time series analysis helped identify:

- seasonal demand patterns;
- revenue volatility;
- stores with higher forecasting uncertainty;
- baseline performance without digital intervention;
- locations where digital tools could have the strongest operational impact.

This provided a quantitative foundation for the consulting recommendation.

## Clustering Analysis

A clustering analysis was conducted to segment stores based on revenue profile and volatility.

The logic was to group stores by operational behavior rather than treating all locations equally.

### Store Segmentation Logic

| Segment Type | Strategic Interpretation |
|-------------|--------------------------|
| High revenue / high volatility | Best candidates for high-impact digital pilots |
| Mid revenue / mid volatility | Suitable for scaled optimization after pilot validation |
| Low revenue / low volatility | Lower-risk environments for controlled testing and training |

This analysis supported a phased rollout strategy instead of a one-size-fits-all implementation.

## Proposed Digital Transformation Strategy

The recommended solution was based on three interconnected technologies.

### RFID for Operational Efficiency

RFID was proposed to improve:

- inventory accuracy;
- stock visibility;
- stocktaking speed;
- replenishment efficiency;
- reduction of manual workload;
- better availability during peak demand periods.

### AI-powered CRM for Personalization

AI-CRM was proposed to improve:

- customer segmentation;
- personalized product recommendations;
- reactivation of inactive clients;
- client retention;
- automated follow-ups;
- sales associate productivity.

### Augmented Reality for Customer Experience

AR was proposed to improve:

- product visualization;
- virtual try-on experience;
- customer engagement;
- conversion support;
- cross-selling opportunities;
- differentiation of the in-store luxury experience.

## Scenario Modeling

The project included a scenario-based implementation model comparing baseline performance with projected digital transformation impact.

The scenario logic considered:

- partial implementation;
- full rollout;
- delayed rollout;
- lower-than-expected CRM adoption;
- stronger-than-expected pilot store performance;
- combined impact of RFID, AR, and AI-CRM.

The model used conservative multipliers based on academic literature, retail benchmarks, and project assumptions.

## Implementation Roadmap

Main activities:

- vendor evaluation;

## Risk Analysis

The project included a consulting-style risk matrix.

Key risks considered:

- implementation delays;
- system integration failure;
- vendor dependency;
- insufficient staff training;
- data privacy and security risks;
- low adoption of AR or AI-CRM tools;
- inconsistent inventory data despite RFID;
- technology obsolescence;
- budget overruns.

Each risk was connected to mitigation actions such as pre-testing, phased rollout, staff training, vendor diversification, privacy compliance, and continuous monitoring.
